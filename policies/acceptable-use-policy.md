# Acceptable Use Policy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2025  
**Next Review Date:** 2026-01-01  
**Owner:** IT Security Team / Human Resources

## Purpose

This policy establishes acceptable use guidelines for organizational IT resources, including systems, networks,
applications, and data, to ensure secure and appropriate usage while protecting organizational assets and
maintaining productivity.

## Scope

This policy applies to all users of organizational IT resources, including:

- Employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Contractors and consultants
- Vendors and third-party service providers
- Interns and volunteers
- Any individual granted access to organizational IT resources

This policy applies to all IT resources including:

- Computer systems and workstations
- Mobile devices (company-owned and BYOD)
- Network infrastructure
- Software applications
- Email and communication systems
- Internet access
- Cloud services
- Data and information systems

## Policy Statement

All users must use organizational IT resources responsibly, ethically, and in accordance with this policy,
applicable laws, and organizational policies. IT resources are provided for business purposes and must be used
in a manner that protects organizational assets and reputation.

## General Principles

### 1. Business Use

- IT resources provided primarily for business purposes
- Limited personal use permitted if:
  - Does not interfere with work responsibilities
  - Does not violate this policy
  - Does not create security risks
  - Does not consume excessive resources

### 2. Security and Privacy

- Users responsible for maintaining security
- Passwords must be protected and not shared
- Security incidents must be reported immediately
- Privacy of others must be respected

### 3. Compliance

- All use must comply with applicable laws and regulations
- Organizational policies must be followed
- Industry standards and best practices apply
- Regulatory requirements must be met

### 4. Respect and Professionalism

- Respectful and professional communication
- No harassment, discrimination, or offensive content
- Appropriate language and behavior
- Respect for intellectual property

## Acceptable Use

### Authorized Activities

Users may use IT resources for:

- Business-related work and communications
- Authorized business applications and systems
- Legitimate research and professional development
- Limited personal use (as defined in this policy)
- Training and education related to job function
- Collaboration and teamwork activities

### Personal Use Guidelines

Limited personal use is permitted if:

- **Time:** Does not interfere with work responsibilities
- **Resources:** Does not consume excessive bandwidth or storage
- **Security:** Does not create security risks
- **Content:** Does not violate this policy or laws
- **Cost:** Does not incur additional costs to organization

Examples of acceptable limited personal use:

- Brief personal email (during breaks)
- Checking personal news (during breaks)
- Personal banking (during breaks)
- Brief personal phone calls (during breaks)

## Prohibited Activities

### Security Violations

The following activities are strictly prohibited:

- Sharing passwords or authentication credentials
- Attempting to bypass security controls
- Installing unauthorized software or hardware
- Accessing systems or data without authorization
- Attempting to gain unauthorized access
- Disabling or circumventing security software
- Creating or using backdoors or unauthorized access methods
- Testing security without authorization
- Unauthorized network scanning or probing

### Illegal Activities

The following illegal activities are strictly prohibited:

- Copyright infringement or piracy
- Software piracy or license violations
- Accessing or distributing illegal content
- Gambling or illegal betting
- Drug trafficking or illegal commerce
- Any activity violating local, state, or federal laws

### Inappropriate Content

The following content-related activities are prohibited:

- Accessing, storing, or distributing:
  - Pornographic or sexually explicit material
  - Offensive, discriminatory, or harassing content
  - Content promoting violence or hate
  - Content violating organizational values
- Creating or distributing defamatory content
- Harassment, bullying, or discrimination
- Threats or intimidation

### System Abuse

The following system abuse activities are prohibited:

- Intentionally disrupting IT services
- Denial of service attacks
- Spamming or mass emailing
- Sending chain letters or hoaxes
- Creating or distributing malware
- Unauthorized system modifications
- Excessive resource consumption
- Unauthorized monitoring or surveillance

### Data and Information Misuse

The following data-related activities are prohibited:

- Unauthorized access to data
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information
- Unauthorized modification or deletion of data
- Data exfiltration or unauthorized copying
- Violation of privacy rights
- Unauthorized data sharing
- Violation of [Data Classification Policy](./data-classification-policy.md)

### Network Abuse

The following network-related activities are prohibited:

- Unauthorized network access
- Network scanning or probing
- Unauthorized network configuration changes
- Bypassing network security controls
- Unauthorized VPN or proxy usage
- Bandwidth abuse or excessive usage

## Email and Communication

### Email Usage

- Email provided for business purposes
- Professional and appropriate communication
- No spam, chain letters, or hoaxes
- No unauthorized mass emailing
- Compliance with email security policies
- No email for illegal or prohibited activities

### Communication Standards

- Professional and respectful communication
- Appropriate language and tone
- No harassment, discrimination, or threats
- Compliance with organizational communication policies
- Respect for privacy and confidentiality

### Email Security

- Strong passwords required
- No password sharing
- Suspicious emails reported
- No opening suspicious attachments
- No clicking suspicious links
- Compliance with phishing prevention guidelines

## Internet Usage

### Internet Access

- Internet provided for business purposes
- Limited personal use permitted (during breaks)
- No excessive personal internet usage
- No access to prohibited websites
- Compliance with internet security policies

### Prohibited Websites

Access to the following types of websites is prohibited:

- Illegal content or activities
- Pornographic or sexually explicit content
- Gambling or betting sites
- Sites promoting violence or hate
- Sites distributing malware or illegal software
- Sites violating organizational values

### Internet Security

- No downloading unauthorized software
- No accessing untrusted websites
- No bypassing web filters or security controls
- Suspicious websites reported
- Compliance with web security policies

## Software and Applications

### Authorized Software

- Only authorized software may be installed
- Software must be approved by IT department
- Licensed software only
- No pirated or unlicensed software
- Compliance with [Software Management Policy](./software-management-policy.md)

### Software Installation

- IT department approval required
- No unauthorized software installation
- No modification of system software
- No disabling of security software
- Compliance with software policies

### Open Source Software

- Open source software requires approval
- License compliance verified
- Security assessment conducted
- Approved through software management process

## Mobile Devices

### Company-Owned Devices

- Company-owned devices subject to this policy
- Mobile device management (MDM) may be required
- Security controls must be enabled
- Device usage monitored
- Compliance with mobile device policies

### Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)

- BYOD permitted if approved
- Security requirements must be met
- MDM enrollment may be required
- Organizational data protected
- Personal use separated from business use
- Compliance with BYOD policies

### Mobile Device Security

- Passwords or biometric authentication required
- Encryption enabled
- Remote wipe capability
- Security updates applied
- Approved applications only

## Cloud Services

### Cloud Service Usage

- Only approved cloud services may be used
- Cloud services must meet security requirements
- Data classification requirements apply
- Compliance with [Supplier Security Policy](./supplier-security-policy.md)
- Approval required before using new cloud services

### Cloud Service Security

- Strong authentication required
- Data encryption required
- Access controls implemented
- Compliance with data protection requirements
- Regular security reviews

## Social Media

### Social Media Usage

- Personal social media use permitted (outside work hours)
- No organizational social media without authorization
- No disclosure of confidential information
- Professional conduct on social media
- Compliance with social media policies

### Social Media Guidelines

- No posting confidential information
- No posting defamatory content
- Respectful and professional conduct
- No impersonating organization
- Compliance with organizational values

## Remote Access

### Remote Access Usage

- Remote access permitted if authorized
- Compliance with [Remote Access Policy](./remote-access-policy.md)
- Secure connection required
- Multi-factor authentication required
- Approved remote access methods only

### Remote Access Security

- Strong authentication required
- Encrypted connections required
- Secure devices and networks
- No access from public or untrusted networks
- Compliance with remote access policies

## Data Protection

### Data Handling

- Data handled according to classification
- Confidential data protected appropriately
- No unauthorized data access or disclosure
- Compliance with [Data Classification Policy](./data-classification-policy.md)
- Data retention and disposal policies followed

### Data Storage

- Data stored in approved locations
- Cloud storage requires approval
- Personal cloud storage not permitted for business data
- Data backup and recovery procedures followed
- Compliance with data protection requirements

## Monitoring and Privacy

### Monitoring Rights

The organization reserves the right to:

- Monitor IT resource usage
- Review email and communications
- Monitor network traffic
- Review system logs and activity
- Audit user activity
- Investigate policy violations

### Privacy Expectations

- Users should have no expectation of privacy
- Personal use does not create privacy rights
- Monitoring may occur without notice
- Activity may be reviewed for security and compliance
- Legal and regulatory requirements apply

### Monitoring Purpose

Monitoring conducted for:

- Security and threat detection
- Policy compliance
- Performance optimization
- Legal and regulatory compliance
- Incident investigation

## Incident Reporting

### Security Incidents

Users must immediately report:

- Security incidents or breaches
- Suspected unauthorized access
- Malware or security threats
- Policy violations
- Lost or stolen devices
- Suspicious activity

### Reporting Procedures

- Report to IT Security Team immediately
- Follow [Incident Response Policy](./incident-response-policy.md)
- Provide detailed information
- Preserve evidence if possible
- Do not attempt to investigate independently

## Consequences of Violations

### Disciplinary Action

Violations of this policy may result in:

- Verbal or written warnings
- Suspension of IT resource access
- Disciplinary action per HR policies
- Termination of employment
- Legal action if applicable
- Criminal prosecution if laws violated

### Violation Severity

Violations classified by severity:

- **Minor:** Limited personal use, first offense
- **Moderate:** Policy violation, repeated offenses
- **Major:** Security violation, illegal activity
- **Critical:** Data breach, criminal activity

## Acknowledgment

### Policy Acknowledgment

- All users must acknowledge this policy
- Acknowledgment required before access granted
- Annual re-acknowledgment required
- Acknowledgment documented
- Failure to acknowledge may result in access denial

### Training

- Annual acceptable use training required
- Training covers policy requirements
- Training completion documented
- Additional training for violations
- Specialized training as needed

### IT Security Team

- Maintain and update this policy
- Monitor compliance
- Investigate violations
- Provide training and awareness
- Report to management

### Human Resources

- Policy enforcement
- Disciplinary actions
- Employee training coordination
- Policy communication

### Managers and Supervisors

- Ensure staff compliance
- Report violations
- Provide guidance
- Support training

### All Users

- Read and understand this policy
- Comply with policy requirements
- Report violations
- Participate in training
- Acknowledge policy

## Review and Updates

This policy shall be reviewed:

- **Annually** as part of management review
- When significant changes occur in:
  - Technology infrastructure
  - Threat landscape
  - Regulatory requirements
  - Business requirements
  - Organizational structure
